SailReady — branding concepts

Same palette as the app. Every logo works at favicon size and in monochrome.

Logo concepts

sailready

A · The Check-Sail ★

The green checkmark's long stroke becomes the luff of the sail — the whole product in one glyph: conditions checked, sail set. Reads at 16px, works one-color, and the check is literally the Go verdict.

sailready

B · The Compass Badge

Sail and waterline inside a bearing ring with a north tick. Most "marine app" of the set — instantly trustworthy, slightly more conventional. Great as an iOS app icon.

sailready

C · Twin Sails

Jib and main over a waterline — green and blue echo the score colors. Friendly and balanced; the most "cruising" of the set, least conceptual.

sailready

D · The Burgee

A yacht-club pennant with the check knocked out of it. Burgees mean membership and identity — nice undertone for an eventual community of skippers. Flag + check = "cleared to sail."

sailredy

E · Sail-as-A Wordmark

Pure wordmark — the A in "ready" is a sail over a green waterline. No separate icon to manage; weakest at favicon size (you'd cut just the sail-A out for that).

Buttons (as now live in the app)

Primary = gradient fill (one per view). Everything else = quiet outline that lights up accent on hover. Destructive = red outline, never a red slab.
